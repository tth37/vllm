Interpretation
  1. Async output copy is the critical optimization.
     Disabling it added about 0.67 ms TPOT at TP=1 and 0.66 ms TPOT at TP=2
     relative to the Docker-in-Docker MP baseline.

  2. Response-side SHM had little measurable impact on this setup.
     The hybrid-SHM ablation and the final full-SHM variant were effectively
     tied at both TP sizes, so the response-MQ SHM optimization is much
     smaller than the async-output effect here.

  3. The dominant gap-closing step is now isolated.
     DockerBE reaches baseline parity once async output copy is enabled;
     full SHM does not introduce a further material latency win in this run.
